Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how the body turns food into energy. Most food is broken down into sugar, also called glucose, and released into the bloodstream. When blood sugar rises, the pancreas releases insulin.

Type 1 diabetes occurs when the body does not produce enough insulin. Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body cannot use insulin properly. Common symptoms of diabetes include increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurry vision, slow wound healing, and unexplained weight changes.

Hypertension, also called high blood pressure, is a condition where the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high. It can increase the risk of heart disease, stroke, and kidney problems. Lifestyle changes such as reducing salt intake, exercising regularly, managing stress, and taking prescribed medication can help manage hypertension.

Asthma is a chronic condition that affects the airways in the lungs. It can cause wheezing, coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. Triggers may include allergens, exercise, cold air, smoke, and respiratory infections.
